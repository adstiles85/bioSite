Interview Questions & Answers

Q: Can you tell me a little about your childhood and where you grew up?

A: I grew up mostly in Kearney, NE. I lived with my mom and dad, but they divorced when I was young. I lived with my mom until fourth grade, then I moved in with my Dad and grandparents.

Q: What are some of your biggest hobbies or passions?

A: I LOVE reading. I like the feeling of getting out of my shoes and stepping into someone else’s for a while. It’s like I get to live another life for a while.

Q: What’s one accomplishment you’re really proud of?

A: I’m very proud of myself for getting my Culinary Arts degree… but, really proud that I have raised my kids to be good people.

Q: How would your friends or family describe you?

A: It depends on who you ask. I can be loud with some people, and quiet with others. I think they’d find me funny, and hopefully think that I am a kind person.

Q: What’s something people might be surprised to learn about you?

A: That I was a teen mom. When I tell people at work that I had my first son at 16 years old, they are surprised.

Q: What do you do for work, and what do you enjoy most about it?

A: I work as a Customer Service Representative at Streck. I enjoy being able to help people. I like it when I am able to go above and beyond for people. They are almost always extremely appreciative of it.

Q: What’s a goal you’re working toward right now?

A: I am working on my bachelor's degree in Business Management and Leadership at Bellevue University. I will finish it by this time next year, hopefully.

Q: Is there a quote or saying that really resonates with you?

A: Well-behaved women seldom make history.

Q: What do you enjoy most about your everyday life?

A: My kids, and my dog. I enjoy watching my kids grow, and the comfort that my dog provides.

Q: What’s something you hope people remember about you?

A: That I was a good person. Even with my faults, I can only hope that I do more good than bad.
